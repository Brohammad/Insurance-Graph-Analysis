CRITICAL ILLNESS RIDER
Rider Code: CIR-2025
Attached to Policy: POL001, POL002, POL003, POL004, POL005

==================================================
OVERVIEW
==================================================

This Critical Illness Rider provides lump-sum payment upon diagnosis of specified 
critical illnesses. The benefit is paid in addition to your base health insurance 
policy and can be used for treatment, recovery, or any other purpose.

COVERAGE AMOUNT: ₹5,00,000 (Five Lakhs)
ONE-TIME LUMP SUM PAYMENT upon first diagnosis
WAITING PERIOD: 90 days from rider inception
PRE-EXISTING DISEASE WAITING: 4 years

==================================================
COVERED CRITICAL ILLNESSES
==================================================

This rider covers the following 25 critical illnesses:

1. CANCER (ALL TYPES)
   - All malignant cancers requiring chemotherapy, radiation, or surgery
   - Includes leukemia, lymphoma, and solid tumors
   - Early stage cancers covered at 25% of sum assured
   - Advanced stage cancers: Full sum assured

2. HEART ATTACK (MYOCARDIAL INFARCTION)
   - First heart attack with specified cardiac enzyme elevation
   - ECG changes confirming heart attack
   - Requires hospitalization for minimum 3 days

3. CORONARY ARTERY BYPASS SURGERY
   - Open chest surgery to correct narrowing of coronary arteries
   - Minimum 2 grafts required
   - Angioplasty not covered under this (covered in base policy)

4. STROKE
   - Cerebrovascular accident resulting in permanent neurological deficit
   - Must persist for at least 30 days after incident
   - Includes ischemic and hemorrhagic stroke
   - TIA (Transient Ischemic Attack) not covered

5. KIDNEY FAILURE
   - End-stage renal disease requiring regular dialysis
   - Chronic irreversible failure of both kidneys
   - Requires dialysis for minimum 3 months

6. MAJOR ORGAN TRANSPLANT
   - Heart, liver, kidney, pancreas, or bone marrow transplant
   - As recipient only (not as donor)
   - Must be medically necessary

7. BLINDNESS (PERMANENT & IRREVERSIBLE)
   - Total and permanent loss of vision in both eyes
   - Corrected visual acuity less than 3/60
   - Certified by ophthalmologist

8. PARALYSIS
   - Total and permanent loss of use of two or more limbs
   - Lasting for continuous period of at least 6 months
   - Includes paraplegia, quadriplegia, hemiplegia

9. MULTIPLE SCLEROSIS
   - Definite diagnosis with multiple episodes
   - Permanent neurological deficit
   - Confirmed by MRI and neurologist

10. ALZHEIMER'S DISEASE / DEMENTIA
    - Permanent deterioration of mental capacity
    - Before age 65
    - Requires continuous supervision

11. PARKINSON'S DISEASE
    - Definitive diagnosis with progressive symptoms
    - Impaired motor function requiring continuous medication
    - Permanent and irreversible

12. COMA
    - State of unconsciousness lasting minimum 96 hours
    - Requires life support
    - With permanent neurological deficit

13. MAJOR BURNS
    - Third-degree burns covering at least 20% of body surface
    - Requires skin grafting

14. BACTERIAL MENINGITIS
    - Inflammation of brain/spinal cord membranes
    - Results in permanent neurological deficits
    - Confirmed by spinal tap

15. BENIGN BRAIN TUMOR
    - Non-malignant tumor requiring surgical removal
    - Causes permanent neurological damage

16. AORTIC SURGERY
    - Surgery to repair or correct aorta defect
    - Requires open chest or open abdomen surgery

17. APLASTIC ANEMIA
    - Chronic persistent bone marrow failure
    - Requires blood transfusions and medication

18. HEART VALVE SURGERY
    - Open heart surgery to replace or repair heart valve
    - Keyhole procedures not covered

19. PRIMARY PULMONARY ARTERIAL HYPERTENSION
    - Confirmed by right heart catheterization
    - Requires permanent medication

20. MOTOR NEURON DISEASE
    - Including ALS (Lou Gehrig's disease)
    - Progressive degeneration of motor nerves

21. LOSS OF INDEPENDENT EXISTENCE
    - Physical inability to perform 3 of 6 Activities of Daily Living
    - Lasting for minimum 6 months
    - ADLs: bathing, dressing, eating, toileting, mobility, continence

22. LIVER FAILURE
    - End-stage liver disease
    - Requires liver transplant registration

23. LUNG DISEASE
    - End-stage lung disease requiring oxygen therapy
    - FEV1 less than 40% predicted

24. MUSCULAR DYSTROPHY
    - Progressive degenerative disease of muscles
    - Confirmed by biopsy and genetic testing

25. SYSTEMIC LUPUS ERYTHEMATOSUS (SLE)
    - With severe organ involvement
    - Class III to V lupus nephritis

==================================================
CLAIM PAYMENT STRUCTURE
==================================================

FULL BENEFIT (100%):
Paid for major critical illnesses like cancer (advanced), heart attack, stroke, 
kidney failure, major organ transplant, paralysis.

PARTIAL BENEFIT (25%):
Paid for early-stage/minor conditions:
- Early stage cancer (Stage 1)
- Angioplasty (maximum 4 stents)
- Early stage kidney disease (pre-dialysis)

Note: After partial benefit payment, rider continues with reduced sum assured 
(75% of original). If major critical illness diagnosed later, balance amount paid.

==================================================
EXCLUSIONS
==================================================

This rider does NOT cover:

1. Pre-existing critical illnesses (within 4-year waiting period)
2. Self-inflicted injuries
3. Alcohol or drug abuse related conditions
4. Participation in hazardous activities (adventure sports, racing)
5. War, terrorism, nuclear radiation
6. HIV/AIDS (unless acquired through blood transfusion)
7. Genetic disorders diagnosed before policy inception
8. Attempted suicide
9. Illnesses arising within 90-day waiting period

==================================================
CLAIM PROCESS
==================================================

STEP 1: DIAGNOSIS
Illness must be diagnosed by qualified specialist (oncologist, cardiologist, 
neurologist, etc.) at recognized hospital.

STEP 2: NOTIFICATION
Inform insurance company within 30 days of diagnosis
Call: 1800-XXX-XXXX or Email: criticalillness@healthinsurance.com

STEP 3: DOCUMENTATION
Submit the following:
- Claim form (signed by policyholder and doctor)
- Diagnosis reports and test results
- Doctor's certificate detailing illness
- Hospital records
- Pathology/biopsy reports (if applicable)
- Imaging reports (MRI, CT scan, PET scan)
- Bank account details
- Photo ID and policy document

STEP 4: CLAIM ASSESSMENT
- Insurance company reviews medical records
- May request additional tests or second opinion
- Independent medical examination if needed
- Assessment completed within 30 days

STEP 5: PAYMENT
- If approved, lump sum paid directly to bank account
- Payment within 7 days of approval
- No restrictions on how funds are used

==================================================
SURVIVAL PERIOD
==================================================

For most critical illnesses, policyholder must survive at least 30 days from 
date of diagnosis to receive benefit. This ensures illness is critical and 
treatment is ongoing.

Exceptions (no survival period):
- Cancer (all stages)
- Coma
- Major burns

==================================================
PREMIUM AND RENEWAL
==================================================

RIDER PREMIUM: ₹3,500 per year (age-dependent)
Premium increases by 5-10% every 5 years

RENEWAL:
- Guaranteed renewable up to age 65
- Premium increases at renewal based on age band
- No medical examination for renewal
- If claim paid, rider terminates (one-time benefit)

CANCELLATION:
- Can be cancelled anytime (pro-rata refund)
- Automatically terminates when base policy terminates
- Terminates upon payment of full critical illness benefit

==================================================
TAX BENEFITS
==================================================

Premium paid for this rider is eligible for tax deduction under Section 80D 
of Income Tax Act. Claim benefit received is tax-free under Section 10(10D).
